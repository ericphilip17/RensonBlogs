5 reasons why you need to capture your child's Snapchat


Over time, technology has advanced at an extremely fast pace. Social media platforms designed for different purposes are coming up every day. Snapchat, one of the platforms, has become quite popular among the young and middle-aged.


Despite the many benefits that come along with these technologies, negative impacts have also been realized, especially to the young. This is why every parent must take an initiative to keep track of their children's online presence. In this article, I’ll guide you through 5 key reasons why it's so necessary to monitor your child's Snapchat activities.


• To minimize screen time.


By monitoring your child's Snapchat, you’re able to track the amount of time they spend online. Through this, you can use restriction measures to limit this time. It's not a healthy practice for kids to be online all the time. There are many other constructive activities you should encourage your child to practice instead.


• Instill morals to your child


On Snapchat, users send each other pictures or videos which disappear after they are viewed. Children can misuse this by sending filthy content such as nudes. This is demoralizing. As a parent, the best you can do is to not only monitor their activity but also advise them on the need to keep off from such content. Doing that will go a long way in building their character.


• Safeguard your child against cyberbullying.


Cyberbullying is targeting to harass or embarrass someone using technology. When using Snapchat, kids may experience cyberbullying in many ways. For instance, a cruel or harsh response to a kid's post or story may drive them into depression. Accumulated embarrassment is very detrimental to your child's mental health. You should, therefore, endeavor to know their Snapchat activity to protect them.


• Keep them safe from bad influence


I'll-intentioned individuals may try to recruit your child into despicable groups such as drugs, cults, and even terrorism! Having a constant check at your child’s Snapchat activities is, therefore, equally important as pulling them out of a burning furnace.


• Protect your kids from child predators.


These are online predators whose mission is to take advantage of innocent children. They may request your child for photos which they then use for impersonation; organize to meet your child for sexual engagements or even plan to swindle you.


To sum up, you don't have to stop your child from using Snapchat. Instead, you should deploy proper measures to keep them on the right track.
