Click Click, my computer freezes, what should I do?


Ever been there? In a hurry to get some job done, or playing some cool game then boom! Computer not responding. It's annoying, right? Well, don’t worry. You are in the right place. The tips below are quite easy and will equip you enough to get your computer up and running once again.


• Give it some time.

Really? Yeah, you read it right. In most instances, the reason why computers hang is because of a particular process is taking too much of your RAM and hence decreasing the performance of the whole system. You should, therefore, give the computer a break of about 5 to 10 minutes. During this time, the heavy process may complete its task, restore system resources and eventually get the system responding.


• Use Task Manager

As an alternative to waiting, launch the task manager using a keyboard shortcut (hotkey) Ctrl+Shift+Esc. In case it fails, try Ctrl+Shift+Esc. The task manager shows you the running processes. If it successfully opens, you can then select the program you suspect it messing up your system, or even kill all the processes.


• Press Ctrl+Alt+Del Keys

This hotkey always comes in handy when your computer hangs or freezes. On Windows OS, It opens a CAD window with various alternatives: lock this computer, switch user, change password, log off, and start the taskbar. Depending on the fatality of the system, you can choose between log off and start the taskbar.


• Press Win+Ctrl+Shift+B Keys

Press the Win+Ctrl+Shift+B Keys at the same time. This keyboard shortcut restarts the graphics card driver. After you’ve pressed, the computer will blink or dim for a short while—about a second or so, accompanied by a short beep sound.


• Last option—Press the power button

After trying out all these methods, the machine can still decide the day was meant to be that bad. But still, all is not lost. This last method will always get you out, it's called the "kill them all" method. Now press the power button and hold on for a couple of seconds. However, you may end up losing some of your unsaved work. The good news is that most programs, for instance, Microsoft programs, have an autosave feature, so the work lost won’t be that substantial.


In conclusion, always avoid pressing random keys on your computer when it hangs or freezes. Instead, try either of the tricks explained above. For the most part, they always work.
