Obesity in Men


Obesity is a medical disorder generally characterized by a high Body Mass Index (BMI). Normally, the BMI of a healthy person ranges between 18.5 and 24.9.


The amount and distribution of fat in the body equally play a key role in determining the Obesity health condition of an individual. As a result, Obesity has been proven to be more prevalent in women than in men. However, its effects are more prevalent in men than in women.


Effects of Obesity in Men

â€¢ Reduces testosterone levels

Most physical characteristics experienced by men such as muscle development, sperm synthesis, and libido are controlled by the hormone testosterone. Its level spikes up at puberty and subsequently, decreases by 1% annually.

As a result, the levels still remain high even in older men. However, they are depreciated by other factors, such as Obesity. Low testosterone levels results to low sex drive, erectile dysfunction and weak bones, a condition called osteoporosis.


o Erectile dysfunction (ED)


Even for men with normal testosterone levels, ED can still be experienced. Only 3% of ED results from low testosterone levels. A California medical study shows that men with a BMI of 28 have more than 90% chances of developing ED.


However, there is still hope for ED men. A study by Italian scientists proved that ED can be corrected by exercising regularly and embracing healthy diets, even without any medical care.


o Fertility Impairment


German scientists associated obesity with low sperm count and reduced sperm motility. Additionally, a similar study was done by American scientists on men between the ages 20 and 30 and similar remarks were realized.


o Low Prostate-Specific Antigen (PSA) levels


The prostate gland enlarges with increase in PSA levels. Obesity causes enlargement of the prostate gland and consequently low PSA levels. However, the low PSA levels may result from dilution of blood since obesity causes an increase in blood volume.


o Prostate cancer


Due to the low PSA levels resulting from obesity, doctors find it hard to identify prostate cancer. Therefore, the late diagnosis explains why it gets so severe. Besides, obesity affects the normal functioning of sex hormones, which often encourages the growth of prostate cancer. Also, obesity steers up secretion of growth hormones such as insulin which is known for enhancing cell multiplication. Insulin-like growth factor 1 has not only been associated with prostate cancer but also colon cancer and other growths.


Generally, the effects of obesity in men are extremely dreadful. Countermeasures should, therefore, be taken to arrest it early.
