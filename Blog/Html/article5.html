The 10 best things about being an introvert


Most extroverts perceive introversion to be a negative trait or a terrible failure. But trust me, that’s a very weird fallacy. Personally being one, today I’ll tell you amazing things you might not have heard before. Read along.


• They make real friends.


Introverts aren’t afraid of small circles, like most extroverts. They value and bond deeply with the few friends they make, ending up in long-lasting friendships.


• Introverts say less “oops”


Introverts don’t jump into friendships, be it a marriage or business. They're very careful and that's why they rarely regret it. On the contrary, extroverts rush to trust even strangers which is quite risky.


• People are keen when we speak.


We’ll be on a discussion and people even forget we are there. But guess what? We command a breathtaking silence the moment we open up to speak. People are more interested to hear what’s coming from the rare roar!


• Introverts intrigue people


By the virtue of their silence, introverts make people get more curious about knowing their nature and the reason behind their unique character. They get people who are genuinely interested to know more about them, and this is amazing.


• They are enduring


When things go south, introverts have mastered the discipline of remaining calm. They sit back, analyze a problem and come up with mitigation measures rather than yelling.


• Have a chance to prove people wrong


People who are turned off by introverted people need to restructure their mindset. The confidence of some introverts will surprise you. For instance, some have the confidence to do great public speeches, and this proves wrong those who underrated them.


• Introverts are conscious of their issues anytime.


Most introverts are avid learners. They spend a lot of time building themselves up, hence becoming more knowledgeable by the day. They're master what is relevant for them, and this often distinguishes them from other people.


• They’re principled.


They aren't affected by peer pressure, they make and adhere to their rules. Therefore, they experience freedom in doing whatever they need and in however way they prefer.


• They overthink


Despite that it’s not always interesting, overthinking gets introverts out of unnecessary drama. Besides, the greatest ideas are bred in deep thinking.


• Are independent beings.


They've disciplined themselves to do most of their work alone, or with minimal assistance. This makes great performers in the job market.


In conclusion, the benefits of introversion are by far more than those of extroverts. Introverts should, therefore, be proud of who they are and rise to challenge extroverts.
